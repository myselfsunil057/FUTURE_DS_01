# 📚 Sales Performance Dataset — Executive Summary

---

## 🧠 Overview

This dataset captures end-to-end retail sales transactions designed for business intelligence analysis. It enables structured evaluation of revenue performance, profitability, product mix efficiency, customer segmentation behavior, and regional contribution patterns.

It is optimized for dashboarding, KPI tracking, and strategic decision-making in sales-driven organizations.

---

## 🎯 Business Objective

The dataset is built to support data-driven decisions across core business functions:

- Measure overall financial performance (Revenue, Profit, Margin)
- Identify high-performing and underperforming products
- Analyze customer segmentation and buying behavior
- Evaluate regional sales distribution and contribution
- Track sales trends and growth patterns over time

---

## 📊 Key Performance Indicators

- **Total Revenue:** $2M  
- **Net Profit:** $286K  
- **Total Orders:** 5009  
- **Profit Margin:** 12.47%  
- **Average Revenue per Order:** ~$399  

---

## 📦 Data Structure Summary

The dataset contains transactional-level sales records enriched with product, customer, geographic, and time-based attributes.

### Core Data Elements:
- Transaction identifiers (Order-level tracking)
- Product attributes (name, category, sub-category)
- Customer attributes (name, segment)
- Geographic attributes (region, state, city)
- Time attributes (year, quarter, month)
- Financial measures (sales, profit, discount, unit price)
- Quantity metrics (units sold per transaction)

---

## 🔗 Business Logic Overview

- Each record represents a single order line item
- Transactions are linked across product, customer, region, and time dimensions
- Product hierarchy enables category-level performance tracking
- Customer segmentation enables behavioral analysis
- Time structure supports trend and seasonality analysis
- Geographic hierarchy enables regional performance benchmarking
